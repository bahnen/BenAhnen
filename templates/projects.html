St. Paul to Minneapolis Traffic Analysis - 2022

In this project, I analyzed west bound traffic data between St. Paul and Minneappolis, looking at what weather patterns contributed to the amount of traffic.

Situation: St. Paul to Minneapolis west bound traffic from 2012 until 2018.

Data: This project will use data that is publicly available from the UCI Machine repository website. I downloaded data from the specified time period and loaded it into R. The CSV files contain data pertaining to the date the traffic was captured.

Question: I wanted to explore and answer, “Does certain weather types or time of the year cause traffic levels to increase or decrease?”

Hypothesis: I suspect that weather types will have some impact on the average traffic, however I believe clear skies will have the highest average traffic volume because more people will want to be outside and traveling.

Liquor Sales In The State of Iowa Post Spring 2020 - 2021

In this project, my team and I analyzed how the COVID-19 pandemic impacted liquor sales in the state of Iowa. Our preliminary research showed that liquor sales had increased by nearly 40% at the beginning of the pandemic, but we wanted to see what was specifically driving that increase.

Situation: Iowa liquor sales from the beginning of 2019 until 2021, provided by the state of Iowa

Data: This project uses data that is publically available from the data.iowa.gov website. We downloaded data from teh specified time period and loaded it into SQL and Tableau.

Question: Our group would like to explore and answer multiple questions: 1) What is the size and make up of Iowa's liquor retial market? 2) Who are the largest liquor vendors in the state by sales? 3) Which retailors have the strongest market position? 4) Which liquor products are the most popular? 5) Which regions purchase the most liquor?

Hypothesis: We suspect that various grocery stores will be the largest sellers of liquor. Our initial thought would be that the Des Moines metro would have the largest sales volume by region due to having the largest population, however because they are not home to any of the state universities we believe that could cause our hypothesis to fail.
